## Coined-Term Paradigm Shift

> "There's a new kind of [activity] I call '[coined term]', where you fully give in to [emotion/state], embrace [trend], and forget that [old constraint] even exists."

Example: "There's a new kind of coding I call 'vibe coding', where you fully give in to the vibes, embrace exponentials, and forget that the code even exists." — @karpathy

Structure: Coin a new term → describe the paradigm shift → paint the before/after contrast

Use when: Introducing a new concept or behavior pattern that your audience already practices but has no name for — giving them identity and vocabulary.

## Escalating Double Win

> "my 1st [product] hit [result] in [timeframe]. So I launched another one using the same strategies... and that one hit [bigger result] in [timeframe]"

Example: "my 1st bootstrapped SaaS hit $20K MRR in 30 days. So I launched another one using the same strategies... and that one hit $61K MRR in 53 days" — @RobHoffman_

Structure: First win → "so I doubled down" → even bigger second win → implicit CTA (what strategies?)

Use when: You have a repeatable system to tease — the escalating results create curiosity about the underlying method.

## Messy Path to Real Results

> "Spent [time] on [humble input]. Built my first [thing] while learning. Made mistakes, launched anyway. Now [impressive result]. That's the '[ironic label]'."

Example: "Spent 15 hours on YouTube. Built my first app while learning. Made mistakes, launched anyway. Now running 3 SaaS at $35K MRR. That's the 'framework.'" — @samuelrdt

Structure: Humble origin → messy middle (mistakes) → big outcome → reframe the "framework" as anti-perfection

Use when: Sharing a founder journey that celebrates imperfection over polish — works well for build-in-public and solopreneur audiences.
